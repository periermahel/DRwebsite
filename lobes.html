Power

Process

Product

Semiconductor Development

Semiconductor development refers to the process of researching, designing, and creating semiconductor devices to meet technological avancements and inductry demands.

Semiconductor Production

Semiconductor production refers to the manufacturing process of creating integrated circuits and other semiconductor devices by deposing, patterning, and doping various materials on a substrate to form electronic components.

Sensor

A sensor is a device or component that detects and measures physical, chemical or environmental parameters and converts them into electrical signals for futher analysis or use.

Supply Chain

Supply chain refers to a network of interconnected organizations, activities, resources, and technologies involved in the production, distribution, and delivery of goods or services from the source to end consumer.

Sustainability

Sustainability refers to the practice of meeting present needs without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs, balancing environmental, social, and economic considerations.

System

A system is a collection of interconnected components or elements that work together to perform a specific function or achieve a particular objective.

Time

Time refers to a measurable dimension in which events occur sequentially and can be used to qualify the duration between occurences.

Wired Communication

Wired communication refers to the transmission of data or signals through physical connections such as cables or wires.

Fieldbus Communication Profile Family :

IRI http://www.w3id.org/ecsel-dr-AT#Fieldbus_Communication_Profile_Family
Description

A field bus is a bus system that connects field devices such as sensors and actuators in a plant for the purpose of communication with an automation device. If several communication participants send their messages over the same line, then it must be specified who (identifier) says what (measured value, command) and when (initiative). There are standardized protocols for this. The first generation of fieldbus technology was developed in the 1980s to replace the parallel wiring of binary signals and analog signal transmission that had been common until then with digital transmission technology. Today, many different fieldbus systems with different characteristics are established on the market. Since 1999, fieldbuses have been standardized worldwide in the IEC 61158 standard (Digital data communication for measurement and control - Fieldbus for use in industrial control systems) and by the IEC 61784 standard. The second generation of fieldbus technology is based on real-time Ethernet (Industrial Ethernet). A fieldbus defines a complete protocol stack over the 7 layers of the OSI layer model. If necessary, not all layers are occupied. Example for first generation fieldbus is Profibus, Example for second generation fieldbus is ProfiNet.
